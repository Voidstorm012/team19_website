üå± Week 1 ‚Äì Our Journey Begins

In our first week, we began exploring how to improve the CommuniMap platform by speaking to stakeholders and designing our first prototype. Our goal was to understand real user needs and shape a solution grounded in evidence.

üîç What We Asked (and Why)

To understand the challenges and opportunities, we focused our stakeholder interviews around:

  • Route Logging: Is it accurate and reliable?
  • Travel Modes: Are the current options inclusive for all types of movement?
  • Gamification: Is it visible and motivating enough?
  • CO‚ÇÇ Tracking: Could it be reintroduced in a meaningful, cost-effective way?

These questions helped us identify what users valued ‚Äî and what the system was missing.

üí° What We Learned

Our biggest insights from Week 1:

  • Don't start from scratch ‚Äî we should improve the existing platform, not reinvent it.
  • Visibility matters ‚Äî if users don't see a feature, it might as well not exist.
  • Gamification needs purpose ‚Äî points alone aren't enough.
  • Emotional connection boosts engagement ‚Äî people connect with things that feel personal.

üß≠ How It Shaped Our Design

This early user research fundamentally shifted how we approached the project. Instead of designing based on assumptions, we began prioritizing based on what stakeholders actually cared about. It reminded us how valuable co-design and user feedback are ‚Äî not just for validating ideas, but for focusing our efforts where they matter most.

üó£Ô∏è What We Asked Stakeholders

Some of the key questions we used to guide our interviews:

  • What frustrates users about the current CommuniMap experience?
  • What features have been tried before, and why did they succeed or fail?
  • What motivates users to engage with the platform?
  • What types of gamification or incentives have worked in the past?
  • What CO‚ÇÇ metrics are meaningful to them?
  • Are there technical or data limitations we should be aware of?
  • Could external services like Strava or NextBike help enrich the experience?

These questions helped us uncover gaps in functionality and spark ideas for new features ‚Äî like reintroducing CO‚ÇÇ tracking with better communication and designing gamified interactions that feel rewarding and visible.

‚ú® Reflection

Week 1 taught us that great design isn't just about adding new features ‚Äî it's about understanding what already exists, listening deeply to users, and finding simple, purposeful ways to improve their experience.
