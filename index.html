What is this project about?

This project looks at how we can use gamification to make the CommuniMap platform more engaging. CommuniMap is a digital tool that lets users log their walking or cycling routes. Our goal is to add fun and rewarding features like streaks, virtual pets, and leaderboards to encourage people to use the app more regularly. We started by speaking with users to understand what motivates them, then built early prototypes in Figma to test different ideas. Through this process, we‚Äôre exploring how to make the app not just useful, but also enjoyable and something people want to come back to.

The Problem

The current version of CommuniMap lacks features that keep users engaged over time. While the platform allows people to log their walking and cycling routes, users often lose interest due to limited interactivity and a lack of feedback or rewards. Without meaningful motivation, even the most well-intentioned users stop contributing, reducing the platform's long-term value as a community tool.

  • Poor Route Tracking: Current solutions lack accurate and user-friendly route tracking features, which affects user trust and consistency.
  • Limited Gamification: Existing apps struggle to keep users motivated due to unengaging or shallow game elements.
  • Lack of Emotional Engagement: Many apps focus only on functionality, missing the chance to build emotional connections that keep users coming back.
  • Unclear Feedback Mechanisms: Users often don‚Äôt know if their progress is being tracked or rewarded properly, making the experience feel less meaningful.

üéÆ What We Learned from Our Stakeholder

01

Current Gamification Feels Weak and Hidden

The app includes badges and a leaderboard, but they are not clearly visible or engaging. Users often miss them, which limits motivation and interaction.

02

Gamification Should Make the App Fun and Habit-Forming

Users are more likely to contribute consistently if the app feels enjoyable, not just functional. Adding playfulness is key to forming habits.

03

Inspiration Can Come from Popular Apps

Tiered leaderboards, streak systems, and group achievements seen in apps like Duolingo were discussed as strong models to learn from.

04

Don't Rely Only on Monetary Rewards

While vouchers have worked in the past, they aren't sustainable. More creative, non-monetary motivators like in-app recognition or unlockable milestones should be explored.

05

Keep It Simple and Visible

Gamification should be easy to find and use. If it's buried in menus or overly complex, it won't make an impact.

üå± Week 1 ‚Äì Our Journey Begins

In our first week, we began exploring how to improve the CommuniMap platform by speaking to stakeholders and designing our first prototype. Our goal was to understand real user needs and shape a solution grounded in evidence.

üîç What We Asked (and Why)

To understand the challenges and opportunities, we focused our stakeholder interviews around:

  • Route Logging: Is it accurate and reliable?
  • Travel Modes: Are the current options inclusive for all types of movement?
  • Gamification: Is it visible and motivating enough?
  • CO‚ÇÇ Tracking: Could it be reintroduced in a meaningful, cost-effective way?

These questions helped us identify what users valued ‚Äî and what the system was missing.

üí° What We Learned

Our biggest insights from Week 1:

  • Don't start from scratch ‚Äî we should improve the existing platform, not reinvent it.
  • Visibility matters ‚Äî if users don't see a feature, it might as well not exist.
  • Gamification needs purpose ‚Äî points alone aren't enough.
  • Emotional connection boosts engagement ‚Äî people connect with things that feel personal.

üß≠ How It Shaped Our Design

This early user research fundamentally shifted how we approached the project. Instead of designing based on assumptions, we began prioritizing based on what stakeholders actually cared about. It reminded us how valuable co-design and user feedback are ‚Äî not just for validating ideas, but for focusing our efforts where they matter most.
